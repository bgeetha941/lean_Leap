# Literature Survey: AI-Driven Career Assessment and Recruitment Systems

This document presents a comprehensive literature survey focused on the technologies, methodologies, and challenges associated with AI in recruitment, career guidance, and assessment. The references are provided in **Chicago Style**.

"A Systematic Literature Review on Artificial Intelligence in Recruiting and Selection: A Matter of Ethics." *Personnel Review*. | AI-driven screening algorithms, Utilitarian optimization models, Automated Video Interviews (AVI). | Significant ethical concerns regarding bias and fairness; lack of human empathy and subjective judgment in the final selection process. |

---

### Key Summary of Surveys (2024-2026)

*   **Technological Shift:** There is a clear move from simple rule-based systems to **Transformer-based models** (like BERT and LLMs) for semantic understanding of resumes and career trajectories.
*   **Methodological Emphasis:** Recent research heavily emphasizes **Explainable AI (XAI)** and **Ethical AI** to counter the "black-box" criticism of earlier deep learning implementations.
*   **Core Limitations:** Across all surveyed literature, **Algorithmic Bias** and **Data Privacy** remain the primary hurdles preventing fully autonomous AI-driven career and recruitment systems.
